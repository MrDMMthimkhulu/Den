Freedom Dayüéâ, celebrated annually on April 27th, is one of the most significant public holidays in South Africa. Key Political Parties in the Struggle

Several political parties and movements played vital roles in the struggle against apartheid and the subsequent establishment of a democratic South Africa.

  • African National Congress (ANC): The ANC was the most prominent liberation movement and played a crucial role in the fight against apartheid.
  • Pan Africanist Congress (PAC): Founded in 1959 as a breakaway from the ANC, the PAC promoted a more radical approach to the liberation struggle.
  • South African Communist Party (SACP): Although a smaller party, the SACP was influential in the liberation movement and worked closely with the ANC.
  • United Democratic Front (UDF): Formed in 1983, the UDF was a coalition of various anti-apartheid organizations.
  • Inkatha Freedom Party (IFP): Founded by Mangosuthu Buthelezi in 1975, the IFP initially emerged as a cultural organization.
